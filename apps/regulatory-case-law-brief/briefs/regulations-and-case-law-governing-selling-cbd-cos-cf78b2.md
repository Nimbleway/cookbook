# Compliance Brief — Selling CBD (cannabidiol) cosmetics in California

**Jurisdiction:** California, United States (federal FD&C Act/MoCRA + California state law: Sherman Food Drug & Cosmetic Law, AB 45 Industrial Hemp, California Safe Cosmetics Act, AB 2775, and Proposition 65)
_Research summary, not legal advice._

## Summary
RESEARCH, NOT LEGAL ADVICE. Selling CBD cosmetics in California is governed by a federal-plus-state stack. FEDERAL: The 2018 Farm Bill removed hemp (Cannabis sativa L. with <=0.3% delta-9 THC) from the Controlled Substances Act but explicitly preserved FDA authority, so CBD products must still comply with the FD&C Act. FDA does NOT pre-approve cosmetics (except color additives) and has NOT prohibited any cannabis/cannabis-derived ingredient in cosmetics by regulation; however, no CBD ingredient may make a cosmetic adulterated or misbranded (21 U.S.C. 361(a)), and any structure/function or disease-treatment claim turns the product into an unapproved new drug requiring FDA approval (section 505) - this is the basis for FDA's warning letters to CBD firms. The Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act of 2022 (MoCRA) added mandatory cosmetic FACILITY REGISTRATION (biennial renewal) and PRODUCT LISTING (annual updates); FDA delayed enforcement to July 1, 2024 and updated the Cosmetics Direct portal for renewals on Feb 11, 2026. CALIFORNIA: AB 45 (effective Oct 6, 2021) created the state industrial hemp/hemp-derived cannabinoid framework under the Sherman Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Law (HSC Ch. 9), with enforcement penalties. The California Safe Cosmetics Act of 2005 (SB 484, HSC 111791.5, eff. Jan 1, 2007) requires manufacturers/packers/distributors to REPORT to CDPH any cosmetic sold in California containing ingredients known/suspected to cause cancer or reproductive harm (regardless of concentration) - California is the only state with this requirement. CFFIRKA (SB 312, eff. Jan 1, 2022, HSC 111792.6) adds fragrance/flavor ingredient reporting (incl. fragrance allergens at >=0.01% rinse-off / >=0.001% leave-on). AB 2775 (HSC 110371, eff. July 1, 2020) requires professional/salon cosmetics to carry full ingredient labeling. Note: California does NOT require general cosmetic FACILITY registration with CDPH (it is voluntary), unlike the new federal MoCRA requirement. PROPOSITION 65: Cannabis (marijuana) smoke (cancer 06/19/2009; developmental toxicity 01/03/2020) and delta-9-THC (developmental toxicity 01/03/2020) are listed; CBD ITSELF is NOT listed. Safe-harbor warning regulations (27 CCR 25607.38-25607.47, adopted 2022) cover inhalation, ingestion, AND DERMAL application of THC-containing products (i.e., cosmetics) and prohibit the short-form warning. A CBD cosmetic with only 'unquantifiable' THC may not need a Prop 65 warning, but AB 45 does NOT make hemp THC 'naturally occurring' for Prop 65 purposes (the business bears the burden). Prop 65 penalties run up to $2,500 per violation per day and are enforceable by the AG, DAs, city attorneys, and private plaintiffs. ENFORCEMENT: FDA has issued many warning letters to CBD firms, including California-based Koi CBD (2019 and 2023), and California firms Latro Inc. (03/25/2025) and Pico IV Inc. (03/06/2025) in 2025 - all for FD&C Act violations (typically unapproved new drug claims). CASE-LAW GAP: No California court opinion specifically addressing CBD cosmetics was located in the research corpus after targeted searches; this is 'not found after search,' NOT 'confirmed none.' The primary enforceable actions found are FDA warning letters (federal administrative enforcement) rather than binding California court precedent. CONCRETE OBLIGATIONS for a CBD cosmetic seller in California: (1) keep THC <=0.3% dry weight to qualify as hemp; (2) register the manufacturing facility with FDA under MoCRA and renew biennially; list each cosmetic product with FDA with ingredients and update annually; (3) do NOT make disease-treatment or structure/function claims (or the product becomes an unapproved drug); (4) ensure the product is not adulterated or misbranded; (5) report to CDPH under CSCA/CFFIRKA if the product contains any reportable ingredient (incl. fragrance allergens); (6) if sold for professional use, include full ingredient labeling per AB 2775; (7) provide a Prop 65 warning if the product exposes consumers to cannabis smoke or delta-9-THC (use the 27 CCR 25607.38-47 safe-harbor warning, no short form); (8) comply with FDA cosmetic GMP guidelines; (9) if labeled 'organic,' obtain CDPH Organic Processed Product Registration and meet the 70% minimum organic content standard. RECENT/ONGOING: monitor CDPH CSCP updates (June 10, 2025 'Cosmetic Ingredients Ban' notice; March 27, 2024 Reportable Ingredients List update with EU fragrance allergen deadlines of 2026/2028; July 1, 2025 fee increases) and FDA CBD policy (Jan 2023 statement that FDA will work with Congress on a new CBD pathway; MoCRA biennial renewals now active).

## Applicable regulations
- **Agriculture Improvement Act of 2018 (2018 Farm Bill), Pub. L. 115-334** (United States (federal)) — Removes hemp (Cannabis sativa L. with ≤0.3% delta-9 THC dry weight, including derivatives/cannabinoids) from the Controlled Substances Act, but explicitly preserves FDA's authority to regulate cannabis/cannabis-derived products under the FD&C Act. CBD products must still comply with all other applicable federal laws.
- **Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act (FD&C Act), 21 U.S.C. § 301 et seq. — cosmetic adulteration/misbranding (§§ 601-603) and drug claims** (United States (federal)) — Cosmetics (including those containing CBD) are NOT subject to FDA premarket approval except for color additives; no cannabis/cannabis-derived ingredient is currently prohibited or restricted by regulation for cosmetics. A CBD cosmetic must not be adulterated (e.g., must not bear a poisonous/deleterious substance injurious under conditions of use, § 601(a)) or misbranded. If marketed with structure/function or disease-treatment claims, the product becomes an unapproved new drug requiring FDA approval (§ 505).
- **Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act of 2022 (MoCRA)** (United States (federal)) — Cosmetic product facility manufacturers/processors must register facilities with FDA and renew biennially (FD&C Act § 607(a)(2)); responsible person must list each marketed cosmetic product with ingredients and update annually; update registration within 60 days of changes. FDA may suspend a facility's registration. Small-business exemptions exist but do NOT apply to products regularly contacting eye mucosa, injected, internal-use, or those altering appearance >24 hours. FDA delayed enforcement to July 1, 2024.
- **California Sherman Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Law + AB 45 (Industrial Hemp), Health and Safety Code Division 104 Part 5, Chapter 9 [§§ 111920-111929.5]** (California (state)) — AB 45 (effective October 6, 2021) established California's industrial hemp and hemp-derived cannabinoid product framework under the Sherman Law, including provisions for hemp-derived CBD in cosmetics. It prohibits adulterated/misbranded cosmetics and sets THC limits and product standards for hemp products. Violations are subject to fines and penalties under HSC Article 1 of Chapter 8 (§ 111825 et seq.).  [source](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?lawCode=HSC&sectionNum=111927.4.)
- **California Safe Cosmetics Act of 2005 (SB 484 / CSCA), Health and Safety Code § 111791.5** (California (state)) — Effective January 1, 2007, requires manufacturers (and packers/distributors named on labels) of cosmetics sold in California to report to CDPH any product containing ingredients known or suspected to cause cancer, birth defects, or other reproductive harm from the five authoritative bodies listed in HSC § 111791.5. Reporting is regardless of concentration. California is the only state requiring such reporting.
- **Cosmetic Fragrance and Flavor Ingredient Right to Know Act of 2020 (CFFIRKA, SB 312), Health and Safety Code § 111792.6** (California (state)) — Effective January 1, 2022, requires reporting of fragrance/flavor ingredients and fragrance allergens on 23 designated authoritative lists. CSCP consolidates reportable ingredients (updated twice annually; 6 months to comply). Fragrance allergens must be reported regardless of intended purpose; concentration thresholds: ≥0.01% (100 ppm) in rinse-off and ≥0.001% (10 ppm) in leave-on products. EU Annex III allergen deadlines of 2026/2028 apply.
- **AB 2775 — Professional Cosmetics Labeling, Health and Safety Code § 110371** (California (state)) — A professional cosmetic manufactured on or after July 1, 2020, for sale in California must bear a label on its container satisfying all labeling requirements applicable to retail cosmetics under the federal FD&C Act and Fair Packaging and Labeling Act (i.e., full ingredient disclosure). A violation expands the scope of a crime under the Sherman Law.  [source](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billVersionsCompareClient.xhtml?bill_id=201720180AB2775)
- **California Proposition 65 — Safe Drinking Water and Toxic Enforcement Act of 1986 (HSC Div. 20, Ch. 6.6) + cannabis/THC safe-harbor warnings (27 CCR §§ 25607.38-25607.47)** (California (state)) — Businesses selling products in California that expose consumers to listed chemicals must provide a clear and reasonable warning unless exposure is below the no-significant-risk level. Cannabis (marijuana) smoke is listed for cancer (06/19/2009) and developmental toxicity (01/03/2020); delta-9-THC is listed for developmental toxicity (01/03/2020). CBD itself is NOT listed. Safe-harbor warnings (27 CCR §§ 25607.38-25607.47, adopted 2022) cover inhalation, ingestion, AND dermal application of THC-containing products, and prohibit the short-form warning. Penalties up to $2,500 per violation per day; enforceable by AG, DAs, city attorneys, and private plaintiffs. A CBD product with 'unquantifiable' THC may not need a warning; AB 45 does NOT make hemp THC 'naturally occurring' for Prop 65 purposes.  [source](https://oehha.ca.gov/proposition-65/about-proposition-65)

## Key case law
- **FDA Warning Letter — Koi CBD, LLC (two letters)** (U.S. Food and Drug Administration (federal agency enforcement), November 22, 2019 (first) and July 18, 2023 (second)) — FDA warned a California CBD firm for marketing CBD products with unsubstantiated disease-treatment/therapeutic claims, rendering them unapproved new drugs under the FD&C Act; products were also marketed as dietary supplements, which FDA has concluded CBD is excluded from being (§ 201(ff)(3)(B)).
- **FDA Warning Letter — Curaleaf Inc.** (U.S. Food and Drug Administration (federal agency enforcement), July 22, 2019) — FDA warned Curaleaf for marketing CBD products with unsubstantiated therapeutic claims (e.g., for cancer, Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, autism), making them unapproved new drugs under § 505 of the FD&C Act and misbranded; FDA cited claims that CBD 'may be effective' in treating serious diseases as a key violation.
- **FDA Warning Letter — Latro Inc. (Pure Dozo) and Pico IV Inc. (California CBD firms, 2025)** (U.S. Food and Drug Administration (federal agency enforcement), March 25, 2025 (Latro Inc.) and March 6, 2025 (Pico IV Inc.)) — FDA issued 2025 warning letters to California-based cannabinoid product firms (Latro Inc./Pure Dozo and Pico IV Inc.) for marketing cannabis-derived products in violation of the FD&C Act, continuing FDA's enforcement against firms making impermissible claims or selling products that do not meet statutory requirements.

## Recent changes
- MoCRA cosmetic facility registration and product listing enforcement began. FDA delayed enforcement from the Dec 29, 2023 statutory deadline to July 1, 2024. CBD cosmetic manufacturers/processors must now register facilities with FDA (biennial renewal) and list products with ingredients (annual updates). (July 1, 2024)  [source](https://fda.gov/cosmetics/cosmetics-news-events/fda-issues-compliance-policy-cosmetic-product-facility-registration-and-cosmetic-product-listing)
- FDA updated the Cosmetics Direct portal (added REGISTRATION STATUS and RENEWAL DATE fields) to support biennial facility registration renewal under MoCRA. Facilities must renew every two years from initial registration date (e.g., a facility registered Feb 20, 2024 must renew by Feb 20, 2026). (February 11, 2026)
- CDPH's California Safe Cosmetics Program issued a 'Cosmetic Ingredients Ban' notice (June 10, 2025), reflecting ongoing state-level action to restrict/ban certain cosmetic ingredients. Businesses should monitor the CSCP Reportable Ingredients List and program updates for any ingredients relevant to CBD cosmetic formulations. (June 10, 2025)  [source](https://cdph.ca.gov/Programs/CCDPHP/DEODC/OHB/CSCP/Pages/CSCP.aspx)
- CDPH Food and Drug Branch implemented fee increases effective July 1, 2025, affecting the Cosmetic Safety Program (including export certificate fees for voluntarily registered cosmetic manufacturers). (July 1, 2025)  [source](https://cdph.ca.gov/Programs/CEH/DFDCS/Pages/FDBPrograms/CosmeticSafetyProgram.aspx)
- CDPH California Safe Cosmetics Program updated the Reportable Ingredients List (March 27, 2024) to add newly listed EU fragrance allergens (Annex III of EU Cosmetics Regulation No. 1223/2009). Manufacturers must report these allergens by EU timelines (2026 or 2028), though early reporting is permitted. CBD cosmetics containing fragrance ingredients may be affected. (March 27, 2024)
- FDA announced (January 2023) that existing regulatory frameworks for foods and dietary supplements are not appropriate for CBD, and that it will work with Congress on a new pathway. This signals potential future federal CBD-specific legislation but, as of the research date, no such federal CBD cosmetic-specific law has been enacted; the current FD&C Act/MoCRA framework continues to apply. (January 2023)
