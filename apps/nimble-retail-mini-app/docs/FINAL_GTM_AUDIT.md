## PART 6 — Conversion audit

Does the experience naturally drive each conversion action?

| Action | Driven today? | Reality |
|---|---|---|
| **Brand searches** | Partial | Only if they reach Run My Brand (3rd–4th section) *and* choose to type. The "absence" payoff is our best hook but it's earned late. |
| **Curiosity** | Yes | The cycling cross-retailer preview + "3 Things" + matrix genuinely create it — *for people who scroll/see them*. |
| **Report requests** | Weak | Email capture is at the very bottom, after raw shelf. Intent has usually expired by then. |
| **Booth visits** | Partial | Booth # is in the header pill (always visible) and sticky CTA — but the messaging is soft ("come see us, we'll pull it together"). |
| **Demo requests** | No explicit path | There is no "book a demo / see my brand live" ask distinct from "visit booth." For remote QR scans (not at the booth), there's no next step at all. |

### Biggest conversion blocker
**The ask is bottom-heavy and soft, while the highest-intent moment happens mid-page and is never converted.**
The single most powerful conversion trigger in the entire app is the **Run My Brand "you're not on page one" result** — that's the moment a brand manager feels the pain. Today that moment ends in a tip ("win page-one presence first"), **not a CTA.** We surface the wound and then don't offer the bandage. That is the conversion bug.

### Biggest conversion opportunity
**Couple the CTA to the emotional peaks, personalized to what they just searched.**
- After the **matrix**: *"This is national. Want to see it for YOUR brand, on YOUR retailers, in YOUR markets? → Talk to Nimble."*
- After **Run My Brand absence**: *"Want to know why {brand} isn't showing up — and where to win first? → 2-minute conversation at Booth #A-42."*
- Capture the brand they ran (`ranBrand` already exists) and stamp it into the CTA + the emailed report. A CTA that says the prospect's own brand name converts far harder than "come see us."

## Persona reactions (brutal, 30-second read each)

**VP eCommerce** — *"Finally, someone showing all three retailers at once. But I already know Amazon share; show me where I'm losing that I can't see today."* → The matrix lands. The hero %'s bore them. They want the absence/gap story faster.

**VP Digital Commerce** — *"The cross-retailer divergence is the real insight. Is this live or a canned demo?"* → Sold by the matrix; immediately skeptical of data freshness. The "sample data" badge loses them. Freshness proof must be loud.

**Category Manager** — *"This is my daily pain — different leader on every retailer, stockouts I didn't catch. Can it do MY category and MY region?"* → Most likely to type their brand. Best-served persona. Make Run My Brand effortless and end it in a CTA.

**Retail Media Director** — *"Sponsored % per retailer and demand≠visibility is exactly my budget argument. But that's buried behind a toggle."* → Their killer stat (paid penetration, what's selling vs ranking) is hidden under "Want the numbers." Surface one paid-pressure line up top for this persona.

**CMO** — *"Looks sharp. What is it, in one line, and why do I care?"* → Skims. Remembers the aesthetic and *maybe* "live retail intelligence." Won't read paragraphs, won't type. Needs the one-line "different shelf on every retailer, live" + a frictionless ask. Most at-risk persona.

**Conference attendee scanning a QR (on their phone, walking):** *"...what am I looking at? Do I have to search something?"* → Highest bounce risk. Sees hero copy + a search box, not a result. If nothing auto-runs, they pocket the phone. **This persona is the whole point of a QR code and the experience under-serves them most.**

## Differentiation as GTM

The sections that *earn a conversation* (rank order): **cross-retailer matrix → freshness/"right now" → Run My Brand absence → localization.** Everything else (hero %, share-of-shelf bars, raw shelf, chatbot) is either table-stakes or off-goal — it can stay, but it must not out-shout the four above.

**GTM framing that should be unmissable on the page:**
> "Your reports show one retailer, last week. We show every retailer, right now — and they don't agree."

That sentence *is* the pitch. It should be the first thing on screen, not inferred from a headline about refresh frequency.

## The remote-scan gap
A QR code travels. Someone scans it, walks away, opens it on the train. Today that person has **no path** except "visit booth" (which they've left) — the email capture is buried and there's no "book 15 min" / "send me my category." **Add one durable, location-independent CTA** (book a slot / email me my brand's report) so the asset converts after the floor closes.
