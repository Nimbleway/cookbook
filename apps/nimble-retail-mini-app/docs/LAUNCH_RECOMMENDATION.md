**Goal being judged against:** get a prospect interested enough to **talk to Nimble.**
**Date:** 2026-06-02 · Companion to FINAL_PRODUCT_AUDIT, FINAL_UX_AUDIT, FINAL_GTM_AUDIT.

---

## PART 10 — Final verdict

# → B. Launch after minor polish.

**Not A (launch now):** the core experience is strong and feature-complete, but three issues would measurably suppress the *one* thing we're optimizing — conversations:
1. The payoff is gated behind a search action (a QR attendee sees a marketing page, not a result).
2. "Demo mode · sample data" / 20s live latency creates a credibility + patience problem at a booth.
3. The conversion ask is bottom-heavy and soft, and it's missing at the highest-intent moment (Run My Brand absence).

**Not C (significant issues remain):** none of the above are architectural. There are no broken flows, no fake-data credibility holes (live mode is honest), no missing core capability. The story, copy, and differentiation work is done and good. What's left is **sequencing, first-paint, and CTA** — hours, not weeks.

**Why B is the honest call:** the product is ~90% of a great booth asset. The missing 10% is exactly the 10% that turns a scan into a conversation. Ship it — after the polish below.

## Top 10 Launch Improvements

Ordered by conversion leverage. Each: **Impact / Effort / Confidence.**

### 1. Auto-run a flagship category on landing
Land on a live result (answer + cross-retailer matrix), not a search box. Let them *edit*, don't make them *initiate*.
**Impact: 🔴 Very High · Effort: M · Confidence: High** — directly fixes the #1 bounce cause for QR attendees.

### 2. Resolve demo-vs-live, loudly
At the booth, run live by default. If offline, relabel "Demo · sample data" → "Live sample · pulled today." Promote the freshness timestamp to a hero-level badge ("LIVE · pulled 2:17 PM").
**Impact: 🔴 Very High · Effort: L · Confidence: High** — protects the entire freshness pitch; one-line credibility win.

### 3. Lead with the surprise, not the answer
Demote the "who owns it %" hero; put the cross-retailer divergence one-liner at the top ("A different brand wins on every retailer — your report can't see it"). Make the matrix reachable in the first scroll.
**Impact: 🔴 High · Effort: M · Confidence: Med-High** — moves our most differentiated content into the 30-second window.

### 4. Couple the CTA to the absence moment, personalized
End Run My Brand's "you're not on page one" with a CTA, not a tip: *"Want to know where to win first? → Booth #A-42 / send me my report."* Stamp `ranBrand` into the CTA + email.
**Impact: 🔴 High · Effort: L-M · Confidence: High** — converts the single highest-intent moment, which today dead-ends.

### 5. Make the perceived live wait feel < 10s
Pre-warm flagship categories; paint the answer skeleton + retailer chips immediately; cap first paint to the 2 fastest retailers, fill the third as it lands.
**Impact: 🟠 High · Effort: M · Confidence: Med** — booth attention is seconds; 20s loses people even with honest progress.

### 6. Cut scroll fatigue: remove Raw Shelf + one duplicate panel from the default
Drop the raw-shelf explorer (most generic, longest) and de-dupe Share-of-Shelf against the matrix. Target 5–6 sections, not 8–9.
**Impact: 🟠 Med-High · Effort: L · Confidence: High** — shortens the path to the CTA; concentrates differentiation.

### 7. Add a durable, location-independent CTA
A QR code travels. Add "book 15 min" / "email me my category's report" so the asset converts after someone leaves the floor.
**Impact: 🟠 Med-High · Effort: L · Confidence: Med-High** — captures the remote-scan audience that currently has no next step.

### 8. Strengthen + reposition the primary CTA
Replace soft sticky copy ("we'll pull it together") with a confident, specific ask. Surface the CTA after the matrix AND after Run My Brand — not only at the bottom.
**Impact: 🟠 Med · Effort: L · Confidence: High** — more shots on goal, harder ask.

### 9. Shrink Ask Nimble; let determinism carry the story
Demote the chatbot to a compact, capped 2-question widget (structured only), or move it behind "the numbers." It's a me-too element that dilutes a sharp pitch.
**Impact: 🟡 Med · Effort: L · Confidence: Med** — removes the most "anyone could build this" interactive element from the spotlight.

### 10. Mobile-QR pass
Ensure the live result/matrix is the first thing on a phone (preview/result above copy); bigger tap targets; reduce stacked cards above the fold; prefill Run My Brand so nobody has to type.
**Impact: 🟡 Med · Effort: M · Confidence: Med** — the QR-on-a-phone case is the literal use case; today it's the least-served.

## What NOT to touch
- The honest live-loading behavior (never show a fake answer) — keep.
- The "3 Things We Found" reframe and the de-consulted copy — done well, leave it.
- The cross-retailer matrix and localization table — these *are* the product. Protect them.
- Don't add features. Every item above is sequencing, copy, first-paint, or removal.

## If you only do three
**#1 (auto-run), #2 (demo-vs-live + loud freshness), #4 (CTA at the absence moment).** Those three move first-impression, credibility, and conversion — the entire funnel — for a day of work. Everything else is upside.
